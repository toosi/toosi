## What I work on

I study how visual perception arises from neural computation, and I use that understanding to build better models of vision. My work spans four connected lines:

- **Generative inference in vision.** How feedback and recurrence in the visual system support perception, and how the same machinery gives rise to perceptual grouping, illusory contours, and imagery. Recent work unifies discriminative and generative computation in a single object-recognition model, tested against human percepts and neural data.
- **Cell-type-specific coding in visual cortex.** Using large public datasets (Allen Brain Observatory) to map how genetically defined neuron populations encode natural scenes, and how coding depends on stimulus statistics and behavioral state.
- **Interpretability of neural representations.** Methods for reading out and comparing representations in biological and artificial networks, including meta-space analysis, prior-guided drift diffusion for circuit analysis, and sparse feature methods on vision transformers.
- **Bio-plausible learning.** Alternatives to backpropagation, including feedback-feedforward alignment and symbiotic learning of feedforward and feedback networks.

A recurring thread is **computational psychiatry**: applying these models to visual dysfunction in psychosis and schizophrenia.

## Toolbox

**Modeling:** deep networks for vision (CNNs, ViTs), diffusion and score-based generative models, recurrent and equilibrium models, robust/adversarially-trained networks.

**Neural data analysis:** representational similarity (RSA, CKA), population geometry, encoding models, large-scale calcium and Neuropixels datasets.

**Interpretability:** sparse autoencoders, feature attribution, perceptually aligned gradients, meta-space analysis.

**Stack:** Python, PyTorch, NumPy/SciPy, scientific plotting for publication (matplotlib, Illustrator-ready vector output).
